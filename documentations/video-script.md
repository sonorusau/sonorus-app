## Voice Direction

### Tone Characteristics
- **Professional**: Conveys medical expertise and credibility
- **Confident**: Asserts the product's capabilities without overselling
- **Approachable**: Warm enough to engage healthcare professionals
- **Clear**: Precise articulation for technical terms

### Pacing
- **Overall**: Medium tempo (approximately 150 words per minute)
- **Hook (0:00-0:10)**: Slightly slower to emphasize problem
- **Features (0:20-0:50)**: Steady, informative pace
- **CTA (0:65-0:90)**: Deliberate and memorable

### Accent & Style
- **Preferred**: Neutral North American or British English
- **Medical Terminology**: Pronounce "aortic," "pulmonary," "tricuspid," "mitral" clearly
- **Emphasis Points**:
  - "Sonorus" (brand name)
  - "AI-powered analysis"
  - "Comprehensive results in seconds"
  - "Professional cardiac screening, redefined"

---

## Music Guidance

### Style
- **Genre**: Modern electronic/ambient with medical/professional feel
- **Tempo**: 120-130 BPM (energetic but not rushed)
- **Instrumentation**: Minimal synths, subtle pulse, clean production
- **Mood**: Confident, innovative, trustworthy

### Structure
- **0:00-0:10**: Build tension (darker tones for problem statement)
- **0:10-0:20**: Introduce optimism (brighter, opening up)
- **0:20-0:50**: Steady confidence (full instrumentation)
- **0:50-0:65**: Peak energy (benefits emphasis)
- **0:65-0:90**: Resolve and hold (brand reinforcement)

### Volume Levels
- **Voiceover Priority**: Music at 30-40% volume during voiceover
- **Transitions**: Brief music swells between sections (1-2s)
- **Ending**: Music holds prominently for final 5 seconds with logo

---

## Total Word Count
**Voiceover**: ~85 words
**Speaking Rate**: ~85 words ÷ 60 seconds = 1.4 words/second (optimal for clarity)

---

## Production Notes
- Allow 0.5-1s pauses between major sections for visual transitions
- Sync voiceover to key on-screen actions (e.g., "Record" when showing recording)
- Ensure music doesn't overpower voiceover, especially during technical terms
- Consider adding subtle UI sound effects (button clicks, waveform audio) at 10-15% volume
